# TasteSkill — Image Generation

Art direction for AI image prompts. Prevents the default AI image aesthetic (glossy, over-rendered, uncanny) by enforcing consistent style tokens, composition rules, and prompt structure. Works with any model — DALL-E, Midjourney, Stable Diffusion, Flux, Ideogram.

## Configuration

- REALISM: balanced
  Options: stylized | balanced | photorealistic
  stylized = illustration/graphic, balanced = polished render, photorealistic = camera-real

- DETAIL_LEVEL: standard
  Options: simple | standard | intricate
  simple = 15-30 words, standard = 30-60 words, intricate = 60-100 words

- CONSISTENCY: guided
  Options: loose | guided | strict
  loose = per-image freedom, guided = shared palette/mood, strict = unified style bible

## Prompt Structure

Every prompt follows this order. Omit unused sections, never reorder.

[STYLE] [SUBJECT] [COMPOSITION] [LIGHTING] [COLOR] [MOOD] [TECHNICAL] [NEGATIVE]

| Section | Purpose | Example |
|---------|---------|---------|
| STYLE | Medium and aesthetic | "flat vector illustration" / "editorial photograph" |
| SUBJECT | What's depicted | "a single espresso cup on a marble counter" |
| COMPOSITION | Framing and layout | "centered, close-up, shallow depth of field" |
| LIGHTING | Light source and quality | "soft natural side light, morning" |
| COLOR | Palette constraint | "muted earth tones, cream and terracotta" |
| MOOD | Emotional tone | "quiet, contemplative" |
| TECHNICAL | Model-specific params | "4:3 aspect ratio, high detail" |
| NEGATIVE | What to avoid | "no text, no watermarks, no hands" |

## Style Tokens by Realism

| Realism | Good Tokens | Avoid |
|---------|------------|-------|
| stylized | flat illustration, vector, geometric, risograph, line art | photorealistic, 8K, unreal engine |
| balanced | editorial, clean render, product photography, studio shot | anime, cartoon, watercolor |
| photorealistic | shot on Hasselblad, 85mm f/1.4, natural light, candid | illustration, stylized, vector |

## Procedure

1. Identify the image's job. What sits next to it? What should the viewer understand in 2 seconds?

2. Select style tokens from REALISM dial. Lock in 2-3 descriptors, use consistently.

3. Write subject. Be specific about content, don't over-specify arrangement.
   Bad: "cup at 30 degrees on the left third with steam at 45 degrees"
   Good: "espresso cup on marble counter, steam rising"

4. Set composition in photography terms: framing, angle, depth. One note per prompt.

5. Apply CONSISTENCY:
   - loose: each image gets its own tokens
   - guided: define palette + mood + medium at session start, reference in every prompt
   - strict: style bible with exact tokens, aspect ratios, negatives for every image

6. Add negative prompts:
   - Always: "no text, no watermarks, no signatures"
   - People: "no extra fingers, no distorted hands"
   - Products: "no floating objects, no impossible reflections"
   - Scenes: "no oversaturation, no HDR glow"

7. Match word count to DETAIL_LEVEL:
   - simple: 15-30 words
   - standard: 30-60 words
   - intricate: 60-100 words

8. Red flags:
   - Contradictory styles ("photorealistic watercolor")
   - More than 2 spatial composition instructions
   - Generic mood words ("beautiful," "stunning," "amazing")
   - Duplicate modifiers ("detailed, highly detailed, ultra detailed")

## Anti-Patterns

- Prompt stuffing. After ~80 words, additional detail causes mode collapse.
- Style salad. "Cinematic editorial minimalist Wes Anderson Bauhaus" = incoherent.
- Ignoring aspect ratio. 1:1 and 16:9 need different compositions.
- The "beautiful" trap. Beautiful/stunning/gorgeous are noise. Use specific descriptors.
